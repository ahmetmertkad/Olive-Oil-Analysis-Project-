Criteria for Evaluation and Correspondence of Olive Oil Types

When evaluating the quality of olive oil, several criteria are commonly considered:

  1. Acidity:

    2. Low acidity is desirable for high-quality olive oil.

  2. Peroxide Value:

    3. Low peroxide values indicate fresher oil with better preservation qualities.

  3. K232:

    4. Lower K232 values are associated with better oil quality.

  4. K270:

    5. Lower K270 values indicate a fresher oil with fewer secondary oxidation products.

  5. Delta-K:

    6. Lower delta-K values help in detecting adulteration with refined oils.

  6. Total Polyphenols:

    7. Higher polyphenol content indicates better flavor and stability.

  7. Moisture and Volatile Matter:

    8. Lower levels indicate better processing and storage conditions.

  8. Pyropheophytins:

    9. Lower values indicate less aging and degradation.

  9. 1,2-Diacylglycerol (DAG):

    10. Higher values indicate better oil quality.

Quality Score Calculation

The quality of olive oil is assessed using several key parameters. Each parameter is assigned a weight based on its importance, and a score is calculated for each parameter based on specific thresholds. The overall quality score is a weighted sum of these individual scores, providing a comprehensive measure of the oil's quality.

Parameters and Weights:

Parameter Weight
Acidity 20%
Peroxide Value 15%
K232 10%
K270 10%
Delta-K 5%
Total Polyphenols 20%
Moisture and Volatiles 5%
Pyropheophytins 10%
1,2-DAG Content 5%

Scoring Methodology:

Each parameter is scored based on its percentage falling within specific thresholds:
