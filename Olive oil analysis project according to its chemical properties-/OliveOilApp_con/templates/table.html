Evaluation Method Evaluation Criteria Benefits Drawbacks
Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy (NMR) Composition of fatty acids, presence of antioxidants, levels of contaminants like free fatty acids Rapid and reliable results, non-destructive, accurate quantification Requires specialized equipment and expertise, high cost
Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR) Fatty acid composition, antioxidant content, contaminant levels Rapid and non-destructive analysis, measures absorption of infrared light May require complex data interpretation, equipment costs
Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) Melting points, crystallization behavior, oxidative stability Insights into thermal behavior, quantitative analysis Requires careful sample preparation, equipment costs
Atmospheric Pressure Chemical Ionization-Mass Spectrometry (APCI-MS) Chemical composition, presence of contaminants, fatty acid composition Detailed molecular information, sensitive detection Complex instrumentation, may require sample derivatization
Solid-Phase Microextraction (SPME) with Nonpolar and Bipolar Fibers Aroma profile, flavor compounds, off-flavors Rapid and sensitive analysis, non-destructive Requires optimization of extraction conditions
Electronic Nose (HS-MS) Volatile fraction, aromas, geographical origin Discriminates between different aromas, rapid analysis Requires calibration, may not provide detailed molecular information
Electronic Tongue Taste profile, bitterness, geographical origin Can differentiate taste profiles, automated analysis May require calibration, interpretation of results
